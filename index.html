Forum Health has strong brand assets — board-certified providers, media coverage in Forbes/Newsweek/Women's Health, 80+ providers in 13 states, and a compelling product story. The website has a solid foundation: HTTPS, structured location pages, a content hub (blog/podcast/webinars), and good navigation. The critical gap is E-E-A-T. As a YMYL (Your Money or Your Life) healthcare site, Google applies its highest scrutiny. Most blog posts lack author attribution, medical reviewer credits, or citations. Service pages have thin meta descriptions, no FAQ schema, and inconsistent H-structure. Local pages are present but under-optimised relative to competitors. This is a site sitting on significant organic potential with a fixable gap between what it has and what it signals to Google.

Critical Gap
E-E-A-T Signals Missing Across YMYL Pages
Blog posts and service pages lack named medical authors, credentials, reviewer bylines, and citations to peer-reviewed sources. This is the #1 ranking risk for a healthcare site in 2026.
High Priority
Meta Descriptions Truncated or Generic Sitewide
Homepage meta: "Visit Forum Health Functional Medicine for personalized healthcare on your journey to wellness." — Generic, weak CTA, doesn't mention geography or key differentiators. Clicks lost at SERP level before the page is even visited.
High Priority
Local Schema Incomplete on Location Pages
13 states, 20+ clinic pages — but structured data for MedicalClinic, LocalBusiness, and Physician schemas appears absent or incomplete. Missed rich result opportunity for every geo-targeted query.
High Priority
Keyword Gap: Commercial "Near Me" Terms
"Functional medicine near me" (60,500/mo), "hormone specialist near me" (9,900/mo), "weight loss clinic near me" (49,500/mo) — massive volume terms where Forum Health's location pages should dominate but likely don't appear in top-5 nationally.
Medium Priority
Duplicate H1 on Homepage
The H1 "Forum Health Functional & Integrative Medicine" appears twice in source — once in the desktop version and once in a hidden mobile version. Confuses crawlers on primary heading signal.
Strength
Media Coverage & PR Signals Are Excellent
Forbes, Newsweek, Women's Health, Men's Health, NY Post, MindBodyGreen — strong off-site authority. Inc. 5000 #1829. These are real E-E-A-T signals that should be surfaced more prominently on-page.

Technical SEO

C+ · Needs Work
Check Status Finding Recommendation
HTTPS / SSL ✓ Pass Site loads on HTTPS. All pages confirmed secure. No mixed-content alerts detected. No action needed.
Canonical Tags ⚠ Check Homepage canonical appears set. However, with 13-state location pages and multiple sub-blogs (e.g. /clinics/texas/the-woodlands/functional-medicine-blog/ vs main blog), canonical risks across similar pages are high. Likely duplicate signals across state and city variants. Audit all location + blog pages for canonical chain. Ensure blog subpages consolidate to primary blog.
Duplicate H1 Tags ⚠ Issue Homepage HTML renders "Forum Health Functional & Integrative Medicine" twice — separate desktop and mobile DOM elements, both visible to Googlebot. Dilutes the primary keyword signal. Use a single H1 in DOM; hide mobile/desktop variants via CSS only (not duplicate HTML elements).
Image Alt Text ⚠ Issue Homepage logo: alt="ForumHealth_Horizontal Lockup Navy" — filename-based alt, not descriptive. Multiple images across pages lack alt attributes or use non-descriptive filenames (e.g. download-1.webp). Pattern likely site-wide. Replace all alt text with descriptive, keyword-relevant descriptions. Logo alt = "Forum Health Integrative Medicine Clinics".
Schema Markup ✗ Gap Minimal structured data detected. Missing: MedicalOrganization, LocalBusiness per clinic, Physician per provider, FAQPage on service pages, BreadcrumbList sitewide, Article + MedicalWebPage on blog posts. This is a major rich result miss for a healthcare network. Implement full schema suite: MedicalOrganization (root), LocalBusiness (per clinic), Physician (per provider), FAQPage (service pages), Article (blog).
Page Speed / CWV ⚠ Likely Issue Homepage loads significant media (videos, carousels, 20+ SVG media logos, multiple .webp images). WordPress-based with heavy scripts. LCP likely affected by hero image/video load. Cookie consent banner may contribute to CLS. No direct PageSpeed data available — requires PSI test to confirm. Run PageSpeed Insights. Target: LCP <2.5s, CLS <0.1, INP <200ms. Likely wins: lazy-load media carousel, compress images, defer non-critical JS.
Mobile-First ~ Check Site appears responsive. However, dual-DOM approach (separate mobile/desktop elements in HTML) adds render weight and risks crawl confusion. Cookie consent banner likely impacts mobile viewport. Audit mobile rendering via Google's Mobile-Friendly Test. Simplify DOM — one set of elements, CSS-controlled layout.
Sitemap & Robots ~ Unknown Sitemap and robots.txt not accessible via fetch in this audit. With 13 state directories + individual clinic sub-blogs, sitemap structure is critical. Clinic sub-blogs (e.g. /clinics/texas/the-woodlands/functional-medicine-blog/) may create crawl budget waste if low-value. Verify sitemap at /sitemap.xml includes all key service + location URLs. Block low-value pages via robots.txt if needed.
Internal Linking ⚠ Weak Blog posts link to service pages inconsistently. Location clinic pages link back to national service pages but lack cross-links between related conditions. Conditions page (/conditions/) and Treatments page (/treatments/) are separate hubs but appear underlinked from blog content. Build systematic internal link framework: blog → service page → treatment page → location page. Use contextual anchor text matching target keywords.
URL Structure ✓ Good URL structure is clean and logical: /services/hormone-health/, /clinics/michigan/rochester-hills/, /treatments/bioidentical-hormone-replacement-therapy-bhrt-for-women/. Keyword-rich, hierarchical, no parameter strings in main pages. Maintain current structure. Ensure new pages follow same pattern.

E-E-A-T & YMYL — The Biggest Risk

D+ · Critical Priority

Google classifies healthcare as YMYL ("Your Money or Your Life") — meaning it applies its highest editorial quality standards to this content. In 2026, a healthcare site without clear author attribution, medical credentials, and citation-backed content is at significant algorithmic risk regardless of other SEO signals. This is Forum Health's biggest ranking gap.

E-E-A-T Factor Grade Finding & Recommendation
Author Attribution — Blog Posts ✗ Missing
Most blog posts have no visible author byline. One exception found: "A New Era of Women's Health" shows "Reviewed by Connie Casebolt, MD, MPH of Forum Health Greenville" — this is the correct format, but it appears rare. Without named authors with credentials on all content, Google cannot assess the expertise of the content creator.
Every blog post must show: Author name + credentials + clinic + brief bio (50 words) + link to provider profile page. Where ghostwritten, add "Medically reviewed by [name, credential]."
Provider Credentials Display ~ Partial
Individual clinic pages show provider bios with credentials (e.g. "Dr. Tara Scott leads this functional medicine practice... board certified in Integrative Medicine, North American Menopause Society Certified Menopause Practitioner"). This is strong E-E-A-T. However, national service pages don't consistently feature provider credentials or testimonials from named, credentialled physicians.
Surface provider credentials on all national service pages. Add a "Meet the specialists" module with linked provider bios on every major service page.
Medical Citations / References ✗ Missing
Blog posts reviewed make medical claims (cholesterol statistics, treatment efficacy references) without citations to peer-reviewed sources. E.g. the heart health blog cites cardiovascular statistics ("About 600,000 people die of heart disease in the United States every year") without source links. In YMYL healthcare, uncited medical claims are an E-E-A-T red flag.
Add citation/reference sections to all content pages. Link to PubMed, NIH, Mayo Clinic, AHA, or peer-reviewed sources for every medical claim. Format as footnotes or "Sources" section at bottom.
About Page & Leadership ~ Partial
The About page mentions CEO Phil Hagerman with background detail. Mission statement is clear. However, the page lacks: a medical advisory board, aggregate provider credentials (e.g. "80+ board-certified MDs and DOs"), clinical outcome data, or regulatory/accreditation signals.
Strengthen About page with: Medical Leadership section, Board of Advisors, aggregate credential stats, years of practice, outcome data (where HIPAA-compliant), and accreditation badges.
Media Coverage / Trust Signals ✓ Strong
Homepage prominently features 20+ media logos (Forbes, Newsweek, Women's Health, Men's Health, Fox News, NY Post, etc.) with live links to actual coverage. Inc. 5000 recognition. Healthcare Business Review award. These are genuine E-E-A-T authority signals — strong and correctly displayed.
Extend these trust signals to service pages and the blog index. Add a "As Featured In" bar on each service page header.
Patient Reviews Integration ✓ Good
Testimonials page exists, homepage shows review snippets with star ratings, review platforms (Healthgrades, WebMD, Yelp, Facebook, Google) linked. Named patient reviews with first name + last initial are present. This is correct for YMYL trust-building.
Add Review schema markup to enable Google rich results star ratings in SERPs. Ensure testimonials on service pages are also schema-marked.
Content Update Dates ⚠ Check
Blog posts in search results show dates (e.g. "May 19, 2023", "October 1, 2025"). Some content appears to be years old without visible update dates. For YMYL medical content, "freshness" matters — outdated medical information can be flagged by Google quality raters.
Display "Last Updated" date on all medical content. Refresh top-performing older posts annually. Add "Medically reviewed in [Year]" to author block.

Local SEO

B · Good Foundation, Room to Scale
Factor Status Finding & Recommendation
Location Page Structure ✓ Present
Hierarchical URL structure: /clinics/[state]/[city]/ is correct. State hub pages (e.g. /clinics/michigan/) exist. City pages have sub-pages for providers, services, and blogs. Multi-level local structure is architecturally sound.
Maintain current structure. Prioritise filling out thin city-level pages with unique local content.
LocalBusiness Schema ✗ Missing
Location pages do not appear to have structured LocalBusiness or MedicalClinic schema. This means Google cannot surface rich local knowledge panels, correct address/hours in SERPs, or include these pages confidently in local results. With 20+ clinic locations, this is a significant missed opportunity.
Implement MedicalClinic JSON-LD on every location page with: name, address, telephone, openingHours, geo coordinates, medicalSpecialty, and URL. Can be templated via WordPress plugin.
Google Business Profile (GBP) ~ Unknown
GBP management not directly auditable here, but is critical for local intent queries. With 20+ physical locations, each needs its own verified GBP listing with up-to-date hours, services, photos, and active review management. GBP completeness directly affects local pack rankings.
Audit all GBP listings: verify address/phone consistency with website, ensure all services listed, add high-quality photos, respond to all reviews (positive and negative), post updates monthly.
NAP Consistency ~ Check
Contact page shows HQ address as "601 South Saginaw Street" — incomplete (no city/state/zip visible in snippet). Individual clinic pages show local addresses. Pharmacy address shows differently (Gilbert, AZ). NAP inconsistency across directories, GBP, and website is a local ranking negative signal.
Audit NAP (Name, Address, Phone) across: website, GBP, Healthgrades, WebMD, Yelp, Vitals, and Zocdoc for every location. Use BrightLocal or Yext to identify and correct discrepancies.
Local Content Uniqueness ⚠ Thin
Many clinic pages appear template-cloned with minimal unique content. E.g. service descriptions on Rochester Hills, Silver Spring, and Knoxville pages read similarly. Google penalises thin, near-duplicate location pages in local results — this is a common multi-location healthcare SEO problem.
Each location page needs 400+ unique words: local area references (neighborhoods served), unique provider bios, locally-relevant testimonials, and market-specific service emphasis.
Telehealth / Virtual SEO ~ Opportunity
Forum Health has a virtual clinic programme but it appears under-optimised for national telehealth searches. The /virtual-clinic/ page likely lacks targeting for high-volume terms like "functional medicine online", "telehealth functional medicine", or state-specific virtual care queries.
Build out the virtual clinic page for national telehealth queries. Create state-specific virtual care landing pages (e.g. "Functional Medicine Telehealth in [State]") for states without physical clinics.

Signal Assessment Finding & Recommendation
Domain Authority (Estimated) ~ Moderate
Formal DA/DR not directly assessable here. However: domain is ~6 years old, has Inc. 5000 recognition, Forbes/Newsweek mentions, and healthcare directory listings (Healthgrades, WebMD, Yelp). Estimated Moz DA: 35–50 range. Ahrefs DR: likely 30–45. Moderate authority for a multi-location healthcare network — room for significant improvement given the PR coverage.
PR Coverage Link Conversion ✗ Wasted
Forum Health is mentioned in Forbes, Newsweek, Women's Health, Men's Health, Yahoo Life, Parade, Fox News, MindBodyGreen, NY Post, and 10+ others. Most of these are likely editorial mentions without a followed backlink directly to relevant service pages. This is a massive untapped authority signal. Many of the linked pages point to the homepage only.
Conduct a backlink audit on all PR coverage. For any mentions without links, run a digital PR outreach campaign to convert mentions to links. Ensure future PR outreach targets specific service pages (e.g. BHRT page, GLP-1 page) not just homepage.
Healthcare Directory Citations ✓ Present
Listed on Healthgrades, WebMD, Yelp, Facebook, Google (per homepage review badges). These are high-DA healthcare directories that also serve as trust signals and local citation sources. 5/5 star rating claimed across all platforms.
Ensure Zocdoc, Vitals, RateMDs, and Castle Connolly listings are also claimed and consistent. Consistent NAP across all directories boosts local authority.
Inc. 5000 & Awards ✓ Present
Inc. 5000 #1829 (2024 list), #1709 (2023 list) — these listings include dofollow backlinks from inc.com (high DA). CIO Bulletin "50 Most Innovative Companies" also included. These are valuable authority links from established publications.
Ensure these award pages link to specific service or about pages, not just homepage. Pursue additional healthcare-specific awards (Modern Healthcare, Becker's Hospital Review list).
Competitor Link Gap ⚠ Likely Gap
Key competitors in functional medicine digital space (Parsley Health, Lifeforce, Cleveland HeartLab) likely have stronger backlink profiles from health media, research citations, and podcast mentions. Without direct Ahrefs/Semrush data it can't be confirmed, but for a national brand Forum Health's link profile likely under-represents its market position.
Run competitor backlink gap analysis in Ahrefs. Identify high-DA health publications linking to Parsley Health, Lifeforce, etc. and pursue those same sources with original data, research, or expert commentary.
